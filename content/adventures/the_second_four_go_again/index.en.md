# The Second Four go at it again

## some time later, in a nearby village...

*"well, that was a bit of a waste of time. sure, we now we have a bit more experience with this exploring thing, but we didn't find any indication of where **Pettic**'s party could be, what happened to them and... well, there are no two ways about it, we got our asses handed to us by some magical beings, or invisible ones, whatever it was that happened there. we were very lucky by being immediately by the entrance, otherwise I doubt we would have survived without weapons or potions"*, says **Julienne** ^[you can read more in the [previous adventure]({{< relref "adventures/the_second_four" >}}) ]^.

a new year just started and this seems like a good time to visit The Second Four again, on a new 4AD session. one innovation that I am trying this time is to use dry-erase pages instead of a notebook. I also printed a bunch of tables to avoid flipping back and forth pages in the book(s) even further and devote time just to playing. it is still a very simplified game (a dungeon crawler), at least at this stage, and perhaps that would make it more evident and prepare my playing style for future games (and the rest of the series).

last time I played was 4 months ago (I wasn't as constant as I told myself I would be), so I might need to refresh my memory on some rules briefly. and, well, let's see how our characters evolve. if I recall correctly, the main points are:

* a 1 is an auto-fail
* a 6 is NOT an auto-success, but dice explode
* selling gems for the stated price is correct

just today, I found outthat [Four Against Darkness - Expanded Edition](https://www.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/550549/four-against-darkness-expanded-edition) has been available since December 20th-24th (roughly). this book consolidates some rules and clarifications from forums and FAQs from the original edition, which I was applying mostly on the last session. I will not use it yet, as it adds a lot of content, but I already got a copy of it for my/your perusal.

## Intro

the party looks at their joint funds: 53gp, and a brooch worth 140gp (they estimate). this one seemed to be part of a pair, or a set, and perhaps something worth keeping, but they are in dire need of money if they want to go out again in search for their family and friends.

they returned to a small village, perhaps not exactly the same one they departed from (adventurer's do not pay attention to such minor details), partaking of food and drinks using previous funds, and considering their options. not sad, not happy, but decided to move forward after some rest. when they wake up, outside, a thin layer of snow covers patches of grass or soil. it was not a big snowfall, but a sparkling of snow dust reaching the ground through the sparse forest in which the village is located.

they spend that morning purchasing or ordering some equipment, regrouping and recovering. they split the funds as fairly as possible (none of them is really greedy, and they are into this adventuring party for their friends and for the long game).

## a door on the mountain side

it has been some hours since they started coming up the mountain slope, first across uneven terrain, but with soft soil and grass, later on rockier. they approach a narrowing of the mountains, not exactly a pass (perhaps just the beginning of a crevice leading to a ravine, or to a cave system), when they notice something strange on the side. they were looking for a place to rest, as the sun was already coming down, and the light of the lantern projected some interesting shapes on the rock to the left of the crack. it looks too uniform, to0 straight, too artificial. they approach it and... *"yes, that's a door. should we try it?"*

sure enough, after they push,  the door opens into a large room with three doors at on the far side. perhaps a meeting point, an abandoned mining operation, a refuge. they enter, ready for whatever it is to come.

**Cordelia** and **Remoura** get in front, **Julienne** readies her bow, and **Porchimwitz** takes out his sling. he will keep his spells for when it is needed. they take the leftmost door.

after being welcomed by some vampire bats that are quickly dealt with, they enter a big room where there is apparently nothing, other than a bunch of doors. perhaps they didn't search properly. following the door south, a small alcove has the statue of a humanoid with a lion-like head. **Remoura** touches it and it becomes to live, roaring. in a couple of seconds it crumbles to pieces, after missing the barbarian and sustaining a good amount of damage. they find 110gp, which **Remoura** will carry. ^[I just realised that I was misremembering the barbarian level, still level 1. and that I handled room #3 as if it were a corridor. let's pay more attention from now on.]^

that was a tiny fight and not enough to gain any experience, merely a warm-up. they go back to the main room from which they reached the alcove, still empty.

moving West... a dragon! yes, a small dragon, but a dragon nonetheless. the wizard stows away the sling and points to the dragon, tracing signs in the air. a lightning bolt strikes next to it, without any damage caused. **Julienne** lets an arrow fly, hitting its side. *"how do you DaAAARE!!! Roarrgh!"*, comes from the mound of scales and flesh, which starts to unfurl. the barbarian hits the scales with her greataxe, which bounces of them. **Cordelia** just misses altogether.

a curtain of fire comes out of the dragon's gaping mouth. only the elf manages to avoid it. it burns, it confuses them, but they are still alive. keeping her distance, **Julienne** releases another successful arorw, and drops the bow, ready top unsheathe her sabre next ^[the RAW say this second attack couldn't be a bow one, but it makes narrative sense to me]^. **Remoura** now strikes true, slashing a big wound where the arrows dented the scales and the leathery skin. the dragon was definitely not expecting all this opposition at this time, and recoils, trying to flee. this time the cleric hits the dragon with her mace, while **Porchimwitz** tries to load his sling.

the dragon escapes, leaving its treasure behind. *"we won! huzzah!"*, says **Remoura** happy with her performance, a new reservoir of courage and purpose found. ^[she levels up to L2]^

inspecting the lair, they find:

* a diamond worth 35gp
* a ring that the wizard identifies as a **Ring of Teleportation** (otherwise they would have though it was a simple golden ring, worth 7gp). they all agree **Porchimwitz** should get this one, being the weakest in terms of health.
* a potion of healing. **Julienne** takes this one.

*"wow, this is very different from the last dungeon. do you think they could be here somewhere?"* *"perhaps not here, there was all this treasure left, ... unless they followed a different path."* *"I think we should continue a bit, after bandaging, of course."*, says the wizard. they rest for a bit, keeping their eyes peeled and their ears open just in case the dragon comes back. **Remoura** performs some push-ups to stay warm, her teeth grinning with a smile.
